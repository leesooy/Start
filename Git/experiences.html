Korean Instructor at Intercultural Communications College Hawaii

I had worked for ICC as a Korean instructor contractor for 3 years. I am fluent in Korean and English in skills of writing,speaking,reading, and listening as a Hawaii judiciary court interpreter. I Assisted to develop the ICC foreign language curriculum for Beginner level 1 to level 6 based on my teaching experiences of Korean courses from March to May, 2019(3 months). My main jobs were to provide learning objectives of each chapter in the text book, introduce classroom interactions such as hands-on and online activities, and create assessment items.

My main jobs as an instructor, I developed course syllabus, interactive class activities, lesson presentations, and assessment/quiz items using educational technology such as Learning Management Platform(Canvas Instruction, Wix website development) and applications( online evaluation form, online flashcard, online journal writing). Each course was held 6 hours a day, 6-8 weeks per session. I mainly taught preparation courses for the Defense Language Proficiency Test(DLPT) of Korean which consists of Reading and Listening comprehension areas using multimedia. To enhance students' comprehension skills, some learning materials of video and audio were modified using software such as a video editor, sound effect editor, and screen recorder. (Jan. 2016 - May 2019)

Teaching more than 1,000 hours of language lessons for a variety of professionals based on their Inter-agent Language Roundtable(ILR) level has empowered my belief to education. Most important thing I have gained throughout these experiences is that I can be also a student learning from my students, co-teachers, and system.

UX Study Assistant

I Assisted the research of the Developing Teaching Expertise @ Mathematics (Dev-TE@M) project which was a collaboration with the University of Michigan School of Education(March to May 2017). Main jobs I did were to collect data of the pilot program which was a set of practice-focused professional development modules for in-service elementary mathematics teachers. Additionally, I recorded teacher educators and professional developers during the real-time seminars (face-to-face) or webinars (using conferencing technologies) in their school, university courses, and their workshops.

The most interesting part to work was to review the Dev-TE@M web site and online modules in order to provide suggestions on how well these fit with users’ needs based on User Experience examination skills that I gained at school. Often engaged into Collaboration with other members from CRDG and Michigan Univeristy to analyze recorded data using codes during online and offline conferences. For data management, I encrypted, uploaded, and organized data to the department main server so that both University of Hawaii and the University of Michigan teams could access in a secure way.
